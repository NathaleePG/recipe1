Vegan Chocolate Coconut Oatmeal Pancakes

Gluten Free, Dairy Free, Egg Free, Refined Sugar Free

What else makes breakfast better than a yummy plate of pancakes? These pancakes are light and sweet and go beautifully with a hint of maple syrup and a handful of your favourite berries.They are super easy to make and only require a few special ingredients. Not to mention, they’re chocolate! So if you’re a chocolate lover like me, you’re going to love these because they’re guilt free!
.
.
.
If it’s nutritious and absolutely delicious, you know it’s going to be a good day.

Let’s get baking! <3

Naturally gluten free, oatmeal is one of the best things you could have for breakfast making it first ingredient on this recipe. It’s a great source of wholesome fibres, carbohydrates, and is also loaded with lots of vitamins and minerals such as calcium, iron, zinc, manganese, phosphorus, magnesium, copper, folate, B1, and B5.

Flax seeds are an amazing source of fibre and omega 3 fats which helps to lower your cholesterol. But besides the fact that it’s nutritious, it could actually be used as an egg replacement. I love to use the Bob’s Red Mill brand because I can trust that it’s 100% gluten free along with many of their other products!

The next ingredient that I add for that rich chocolatey goodness is raw cacao powder! I love adding these to my pancakes because it satisfies my chocolate cravings but still provides me with an awesome source of iron, magnesium, potassium, phosphorus and healthy enzymes.

Moving on to the wet ingredients, the only two ingredients you need are one banana and coconut milk! They are both a great source of potassium, magnesium, phosphorus and calcium! The banana on one hand, provides the sweetness for these pancakes so the riper your banana is, the sweeter. Coconut milk on the other hand is great for providing B and C vitamins and iron. I recommend using low fat coconut milk for this recipe.

Difference between baking soda and baking powder? Well, baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) is the stabilizer to all your baked goods while the baking powder (sodium bicarbonate + a dry acid) helps them rise. Now that you know the difference, you won’t ever make that mistake of substituting one for the other again ;)

Mash mash mash! Simple ain’t it? Just make sure I don’t see any clumps in the bowl or I’ll get a tiny bit upset ;) Just kidding, you just want a really smooth batter that’s all!

Add in the coconut to the mashed bananas… Then MIX!!

Then add in the wet mixture to the dry!

After you mixed everything thoroughly together you should get something like this. Don’t worry about over mixing anything since it’s a gluten free recipe ;)

This recipe should give you exactly 6 pancakes. I designed it so that it’s good for one person but if you want more than that, just double or triple or etc, the recipe. I used 2 and 1/2 tbsps for each pancake!

After giving the baking tray a few light taps at the bottom to flatten our the pancakes, I let them bake in the oven for 13 beautiful minutes at 350 degrees Fahrenheit!

The recipe will ask for oatmeal flour and throughout these pictures I actually don’t show that! But don’t worry, I did some magic to fix it all in the end ;) but to make your life easier, just pulse your oatmeal in a blender and VOILA! You have oatmeal flour :)

Okay okay, now here’s the actual recipe :) Enjoy!

Ingredients:
1/3 cup of gluten-free oatmeal flour
1 tbsp gluten-free flax seed meal
1 tbsp. gluten-free cacao powder
1 medium sized and ripened banana
1/3 cup of canned coconut milk
1/2 tsp of baking soda
¼ tsp gluten-free baking powder
1-2 tsp maple syrup
**Berries of your choice (I used blueberries and black berries because they were sweet and in season)

    Instructions:

  1. First, preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit and line your baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. After setting the trays aside, mash the ripe banana until smooth with a fork in a large bowl. Then add the coconut milk and whisk the two ingredients together.
  3. Next, incorporate the oatmeal flour, flax seeds, and cacao powder, then mix.

    TIP: Add the baking soda and baking powder at the very end so they are at their peak of their magic when you bake them!

  4. Your batter should make 6 pancakes (2 and a half tablespoons each)
  5. Let pancakes bake untouched for 12-13 minutes. Do not be tempted to open up the oven because this can disrupt the air circulation during the cooking process!
  6. Plate your pancakes and top them off with your favourite berries and maple syrup.
